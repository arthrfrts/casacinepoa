SEXO & BEETHOVEN - O REENCONTRO
(Sex & Beethoven, the Return)
(35 mm, 16 min, color, 1997)
 
Two straight guys (but not that straight) invite two naïve prostitutes (but not that naïve) for a night of sex. In the preliminary conversation, some poetry, some philosophy and heavy doses of vulgarity, all with Beethoven playing in the background. But when it is time for sex, the truth comes up.

Reviews

"Sex & Beethoven - The Reunion, by Carlos Gerbase, is an erotic-intellectual romp involving two friends and two prostitutes they invite for a heady private party. Anarchic, libertarian, in the style of Gerbase, one of the talents of the south."
(Luiz Zanin Oricchio, O Estado de São Paulo, 23/07/97 )
http://www.estado.estadao.com.br/edicao/pano/97/07/23/ca2309.html

"Those who think Brazilian films have been too prudish lately will have a great time seeing Sex & Beethoven - The Reunion: shown at the last Gramado Film Festival, the film had many viewers astonished at its obscenity, present already in the first dialogue, to the point that only a few viewers realize that it is a comedy of manners, absolutely in tune with the urban culture of our times. (...) At a certain point, for instance, one of the guys says: 'Do you remember Jocasta, Oedipus' mother?' The girl answers: 'Oh, you think I look like a film star?' There are jokes that are so crude that they are irresistible. With his head between Luciene Adami's legs, one of the guys asks: 'You wear Phebo Patchouli, don't you?' And on it goes. Or in."
(José Geraldo Couto, FOLHA DE SÃO PAULO, 25/08/97)
