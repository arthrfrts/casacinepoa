PRESS REVIEWS OF 
CASA DE CINEMA’S SHORTS

"Be moved by the story of a tomato (Ilha das Flores (Island of Flowers), 1989, by Jorge Furtado) or by the memories of an eighty-year old woman (Dona Cristina perdeu a memória (Dona Cristina lost her memory), 2002, by Ana Luiza Azevedo). Discover why a paralyzed woman hires a detective to follow her husband (Deus Ex-Machina, 1995, by Carlos Gerbase) or what makes a prisoner face his guards (O dia em que Dorival encarou a guarda (The day Dorival faced the guard), 1986, by Furtado and José Pedro Goulart). More than short curious stories, the Casa de Cinema’s Shorts DVD set makes the great work by the short movie makers from Rio Grande do Sul available to a large audience. These are films that helped form a whole generation of young Brazilian filmmakers".
(SHORT BUT STRIKING, Carlos Heli de Almeida, Jornal do Brasil, 13/12/05)


"The magnificent Casa de Cinema’s Shorts boxed set collects a sample of the best in Brazilian shorts. Casa de Cinema de Porto Alegre presents the work of directors such as Ana Luiza Azevedo, Carlos Gerbase and Jorge Furtado in four DVDs. Precious material to understand the effectiveness of a genre".
(Revista Bravo, janeiro de 2006.)


"What brings three filmmakers as different as these together, besides talent, is critical humanism, the same awareness that ‘men make their own history, but not as they want to, but according to circumstances inherited and transmitted by the past’, as a certain bearded nineteenth century German philosopher, now sadly forgotten, once said. "In its best moments, Casa de Cinema has been reaching an ideal balance between technical competence, critical eye, invention and entertainment. The "Casa de Cinema’s Shorts" boxed set documents this journey, begun in 1987, and made also of failures and mistakes, like any journey worth the while".
(José Geraldo Couto, Folha de São Paulo, 27/12/05)


"Since it began almost twenty years ago, Casa de Cinema de Porto Alegre has become a standard for audiovisual production in Brazil. Now it is releasing a four-disc DVD boxed set with shorts that made history".
(FILMMAKERS FROM RS TEACH THE ART OF BEING CONCISE, Luiz Carlos Merten, O Estado de São Paulo, 05/12/2006)

 
"The four discs in this set contain 38 fiction and documentary shorts and television specials. The first one thousand units sold also include the episodes 'Anchietanos' and 'Meia Encarnada Dura de Sangue' (Blood-hardened Scarlet Sock) from Globo TV network series 'Comédias da Vida Privada' (Comedies of Private Life) and 'Brava Gente' (Brave People). Too much good stuff for such short space".
(LANGUAGE PEARLS AND HUMANISTIC LESSONS, Luiz Zanin Oricchio, O Estado de São Paulo, 05/12/2006)

"Brazilian cinema lives on a lean diet and for the last twenty years it has gone through all possible stages in blowing up and shrinking down. Since, 1987, however, Rio Grande do Sul hasn’t been able to complain about slim pickings. That was when Casa de Cinema de Porto Alegre came along, formed by moviemakers who started working together in the early eighties, and who have supplied the market with quality movies produced and distributed by the company".
(THE SMALLEST BIGGEST BRAZILIAN MOVIES, Veja Rio)


"C'est avec un plaisir non dissimulé que nous accueillons l'edition en DVD, non seulement de l'integrale des courts métrages de Jorge Furtado mais aussi celle des courts de la Casa de Cinema (...), société de production collective, trés active dans le sud du Brésil, à Porto Alegre. (...) Il est important d'en signaler la variété de genres et de tons: de la comédie au documentaire en passent par le plus kitsch du film historique comme par des propos révoltés (sur l'avortement dans VENTRE LIVRE d'Azevedo ou sur la mémorie dans MEMÓRIA de Roberto Henkin). La dénonciation politique côtoie le simple divertissement offert par les épisodes de feuilleton-télé; le fim de montage s'oppose à la ficción axée sur le sexe (comme dans certains films de Gerbase)."
(COMME À LA MAISON, revista BREF, Paris, mai-jun 2006)
